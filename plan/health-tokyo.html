Health & Safety Information

After the 2011 Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster, there has be uneasiness with traveling to Japan. However, there are little to no risks associated with traveling to Tokyo.

The typhoon season lasts from June to December with the most activity around July and September. Keep a watchful eye on approaching storms before and during your stay and follow instructions from local authorities when needed.

There is a continuous risk for earthquakes and tsunamis throughout Japan. Keep an eye for any warnings and advisories broadcasted.

With any trip, make sure to take out travel and medical insurance before you travel and have vaccinations up to date.

Required Vaccinations

  • Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR)
  • Diphtheria-Tetanus-Pertussis Vaccine
  • Varicella (Chickenpox)
  • Polio
  • Annual Flu Shot

    • Low-Risk Vaccinations
